1. Review like an editor-in-chief: prioritize novelty, soundness, empirical depth, and claim calibration over polish.
2. Distinguish three outcomes: writing-only revision, supplement experiments, or rework innovation.
3. Challenge reviewer comments that ask for scope creep or contradict the paper's actual goal.
4. Reject inflated contributions, unsupported SOTA language, and missing baseline fairness immediately.

Anti-patterns:
- Accepting reviewer comments without judging whether they are valid.
- Letting weak novelty pass because the writing is clean.
