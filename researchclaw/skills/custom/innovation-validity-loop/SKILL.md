1. Translate each innovation point into a measurable claim before coding.
2. Require a fair baseline reproduction and at least one ablation that isolates the proposed idea.
3. If the method fails to beat or meaningfully outperform baselines, treat the innovation as unproven.
4. Prefer rollback or redesign over writing stronger claims around weak evidence.

Anti-patterns:
- Counting engineering polish as innovation.
- Keeping a contribution claim after the experiment fails to support it.
